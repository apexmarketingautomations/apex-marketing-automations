I want to upgrade the Workflow Template Library by rewriting the highest-value templates with elite, production-grade conversion copy.

This is not about changing the workflow engine.
This is not about adding new backend infrastructure.
This is about making the template copy itself feel premium, high-converting, and immediately usable.

Goal
Rewrite the top 10 highest-value templates so the default copy feels like something a strong agency or growth operator would actually deploy.

What I want improved
The current/future template copy must not sound generic, robotic, timid, or placeholder-like.

I do NOT want copy like:
- “Hi, thanks for your interest.”
- “Please let us know if you’d like to proceed.”
- “We wanted to follow up with you.”

I want copy that is:
- channel-native
- concise where appropriate
- conversion-oriented
- human-sounding
- strong on CTA
- not spammy
- not cringe
- realistic for SMS, DM, email, WhatsApp, and follow-up ladders

Priority templates to rewrite first
1. FB Lead Form → DM + SMS Nurture
2. Missed Call Text-Back + AI Callback
3. Speed-to-Lead Instant Follow-Up
4. New Lead Multi-Touch Follow-Up
5. Appointment Confirmation + Reminder Sequence
6. No-Show Recovery

10. Abandoned Cart Recovery

Requirements
For each of these templates:
- keep the workflow logic intact unless a copy-driven improvement clearly suggests a better step order
- rewrite all user-facing default copy across all steps
- use strong but realistic placeholders such as:
  - {{leadName}}
  - {{businessName}}
  - {{bookingLink}}
  - {{offer}}
  - {{reviewLink}}
  - {{locationName}}
- tailor the tone to the channel:
  - SMS should be short and direct
  - DM should feel conversational and natural
  - email can be more detailed
  - WhatsApp should feel personal, not corporate
  - reminder copy should escalate appropriately without sounding desperate

Copy standards
- reduce robotic politeness
- increase clarity and response likelihood
- lead with relevance
- make the next step obvious
- use curiosity or specificity where appropriate
- never sound like filler
- never sound like a placeholder
- avoid stiff corporate language

I also want each rewritten template to include:
- a short explanation of why the copy is stronger
- the intended conversion goal
- the audience/use case it is best for
