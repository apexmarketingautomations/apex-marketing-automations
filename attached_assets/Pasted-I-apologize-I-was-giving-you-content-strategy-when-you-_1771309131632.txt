### 🔱 1. The "Corridor" Schema (The SEO Signal)

This is the code you drop into the `<head>` of your website (in `layout.tsx` or `index.html`). It defines your business as a "Service Area Business" covering every major city in your target zone.

```html
<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "ProfessionalService", 
  "name": "Apex Automation Systems",
  "url": "https://YOUR-SITE.com",
  "description": "Automated client acquisition and property intelligence for Florida business owners.",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "addressLocality": "Fort Myers",
    "addressRegion": "FL",
    "postalCode": "33901",
    "addressCountry": "US"
  },
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": "26.6406",
    "longitude": "-81.8723"
  },
  "areaServed": [
    {
      "@type": "City",
      "name": "Orlando",
      "sameAs": "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orlando,_Florida"
    },
    {
      "@type": "City",
      "name": "Tampa",
      "sameAs": "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tampa,_Florida"
    },
    {
      "@type": "City",
```

### 🔱 2. The Geo-Sitemap (KML File)

This is the "Old School" Geo trick that works for map-based searches. You create a file called `locations.kml` in your `public/` folder. This gives Google Earth and Maps your exact coordinates.

**File:** `public/locations.kml`

```xml
<?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?>
<kml xmlns="http://www.opengis.net/kml/2.2">
  <Placemark>
    <name>Apex Automation HQ</name>
    <description>Service Area: Orlando, Tampa, Sarasota, Fort Myers, Naples</description>
    <Point>
      <coordinates>-81.8723,26.6406,0</coordinates>
    </Point>
  </Placemark>
</kml>

```

Then, add this line to your **Robots.txt** file so Google finds it:
`Sitemap: https://YOUR-SITE.com/locations.kml`

### 🔱 3. The "Meta-Geo" Tags

Add these simple tags to your `<head>` section. They are the "coordinates" for your digital footprint.

```html
<meta name="geo.region" content="US-FL" />
<meta name="geo.placename" content="Fort Myers" />
<meta name="geo.position" content="26.6406;-81.8723" />
<meta name="ICBM" content="26.6406, -81.8723" />

```

### 🔱 Why this gets Business Owners

When a Business Owner in Tampa searches **"Automation services near me"** or **"Business scaling Florida"**:

1. Google looks at your code.
2. It sees the **`areaServed`** list includes "Tampa."
3. It matches your **`ProfessionalService`** schema.
4. It ranks you in the **Local Pack** (the map results) even though you are in Fort Myers, because you explicitly told it you serve Tampa.
